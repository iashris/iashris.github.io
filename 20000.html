20000 Blocks

ABOUT

20,000 Blocks is a project that questions if gameplay be used to guide groups of non-experts through the creation of collaborative architecture designs. Players build structures in a virtual world based on the game Minecraft with the goal to reach the top platform before the opponent. The result of the gameplay were inhabitable structures connecting to areas across an obstacle such as a railroad, a river or an existing building. A robot builds the model using physical blocks as the game is played following real-time stress test in Rhino+Grasshopper.

PLACE

SmartGeometry 2016, Chalmers University of Technology, Sweden

COLLABORATORS

Ashris, Alexander Gosta and Samuel Eliasson

PROJECT LEAD

Digital Design Unit, TU Darmstadt.

The game can either be played as a competitive two player game or a collaborative four player game. The players are divided by a high wall with a platform on top. If competitive, the goal is to reach the top platform before your opponent. If collaborative, you play in teams of two and the goal is to connect your bridge before the opponent team does.
